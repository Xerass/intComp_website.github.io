ABOUT US

The "Redbull"  of the 40s

Our story began in 1940 during the Battle of France. Allied troops that were wounded in battle were tended to; bandaged and offered food, and most importantly, tea. Due to the shortage of troops, failed conscription efforts, and the continuing advance of Nazi forces, the French army had to make the hard decision to send in all its available personnel, also including the wounded. To give allied troops the “strength” or “morale boost” that they need and mimic the effect that caffeine-infused military gum had, we started adding a pinch of certain helpful ingredients to the tea that we provide to them, such as aviation gasoline, coarse-grade gunpowder, brake fluid, and most importantly, whole milk for a creamy and well-rounded taste. This slight variation of ingredients proved to be of great assistance to allied forces. From almost three quarters of overrun French territory, the allies managed to push the Nazis back as far east as Chaumont; 420 miles from the France-Germany border. This bought enough time for British forces to arrive, which resulted in the complete liberation of the French mainland. The British who fought alongside commended our forces for their dexterity and unrelenting energy in battle. Some were stunned at reports of French troops who were allegedly dodging bullets by bending over backwards and “one-tapping” the enemy forces without using their optical sights, whatever that means. An unnamed British soldier also describes his experience with another unnamed French soldier who reputedly dug up landmines with his bare hands after finishing a cup of our tea.

A Near Miss with Bankruptcy

The favorable results our tea had on the troops had prompted us to retain these special combination of ingredients as we expanded further into the beverage industry in post-war 1950s. However, coincidentally when Le Gout de Leau had started brewing and serving customers, there had been a surge of cases of diseases such as lead poisoning, malignancy, and the like. The cause of this occurrence was unknown. Experts from that time pointed their fingers at our tea and its ingredients. Eventually, multiple lawsuits had been filed against LGL for the use of “harmful” substances that had been correlated to the unusual surge of cases of various diseases in France. I mean, Coca-Cola literally used to put some “Pure Colombian” in their drinks back then, so why can’t we add high octane to ours? After several months of court hearings, LGL had unfortunately lost and was ordered to pay a fine amounting to more than half of the company’s total value and was prohibited the use of the ingredients necessary for its well-known signature blend of rich and punchy taste. Nonetheless, this didn’t stop us from continuing our services. We started using friendlier (boring) ingredients as substitutes to the prohibited ones. We initially thought that this would be the downfall of our business. We were wrong. We had received a lot of feedback from our customers, most of which were praises about how they liked us experimenting with new blends and flavors. LMAO, they didn’t notice the government screwed us. We would’ve still wanted to provide our customers the original flavor that was responsible for keeping France from becoming a part of Germany. But, hey, at least our production costs went down. Besides, acquiring 10,000 gallons of aviation fuel and storing it is a lot of work.

Suffering From Success

Twenty years after the unjust accusations were made against the company, we had managed to bounce back where almost all of France had heard of Le Gout de Leau. Five more years later and LGL had gained popularity around the world in places such as the West and most notably in Southeast Asia. In the early 90s, our tea masters came up with a new concept of tea served cold with a variety of toppings to choose from. We initially thought of reviving our signature blend by using mothballs, as our primary topping. However, things wouldn’t work out well if we got sued again so we used Tapioca pearls or boba instead…How boring. Le Gout de Leau then went on to become one of the earliest cafes to offer bubble tea. With increasing demand for bubble tea in urban areas, and bubble tea becoming a requirement for almost every Instagram post, Le Gout de Leau gained even more popularity. As of 2023, almost every major city around the world has a Le Gout de Leau café, most importantly in Pyongyang, North Korea which is the only branch of ours left that still serves the tea with the recipe that started it all. If you’re still reading this then we’re guessing you’re somewhat interested in our beverages.

FAQ

Common questions

Here are some of the most common questions that we get.
What is boba tea? Boba tea, also known as bubble tea, is a Taiwanese drink that typically consists of tea, milk, and tapioca pearls. Well... Yeah, the Taiwanese had the original idea but we got the patent first, soooo.... :P
Do you offer dairy-free options? Yes, we offer dairy-free alternatives such as almond milk and coconut milk for our boba tea.
Are there any plans to bring your renowned recipe back or at least make it available internationally? Unfortunately, no. Assemblage original de Goût de Leau is exclusive to our branch located all the way over in the Democratic People's Republic of Korea. You will have to travel to Pyongyang if you really want to try our legendary blend.
Can I customize the sweetness level of my boba tea? Absolutely! Unlike other cafes who offer boba tea, we've fine-tuned our sugar preferences to cater the needs of our customers. Our proprietary sugar content adjustment system called GlucoLevel™ lets you choose between sweetness levels such as "Diabetes" and "Tastes like Water" .
Do you have vegan options? No. Go away.
