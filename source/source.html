The Origins of Our Exquisite Ingredients

Each drink we brew contains the perfect blend of premium ingredients sourced from different countries around the world. At the present time, Le Gout de Leau imports ingredients from over 30 countries. This is simply the secret to our highly sought-after flavor and aroma.
As much as it excites us to reveal the constituents that made our beverages well-known around the globe, sadly we cannot list all of them due to the vast amount of ingredients we use and some of them happen to be company secrets, only known to a select number of persons within our company. Nonetheless, here are the ingredients that make up most of our tea's composition. 

Sugarcanes from Hokkaido, Japan

Harvested fresh from the plains of the land of the Land of The Rising Sun, it is guaranteed that all of our boba tea selections are naturally sweetened. Our sugarcane farmers work tirelessly, selecting carefully which canes to harvest across a 10-million acre field.

Chocolate Synthesized from the Amazon Rainforest's Cacao Trees

Like our sweetener, we prefer to make our own chocolate as we are committed to delivering only the best to our customers. The rich taste of our chocolate-flavored drinks is unlike any other. The Cacao beans that we use to create our chocolate are usually located deep into the dense jungle of the Amazon, which requires highly-skilled workers to harvest. These beans are among our list of the most difficult and dangerous ingredients to harvest since there have been instances where some of workers have gone missing or have not returned during their routine harvest.

Whole Milk extracted from Normande Cows in Normandy, France

Located roughly 350 miles North from our HQ in Bordeaux, Normandy is home to one of our biggest ingredient caches and processing facilities. A single cow roughly produces up to 8 gallons of milk a day and in Normandy, there are over a thousand healthy cattle ready for milking. Since it is a highly perishable good, it is shipped over to us via air freight, which is a pricier alternative to land transport.

Coffee Beans from Minas Gerais, Brazil

Our coffee flavors for coffee enthusiasts also deserve a proprietary and high quality blend! We chose the state of Minas Gerais as our source of coffee ingredients because of the favorable climate and the locals' dedication from the harvest to the export process.

Black Tea from Assam, India

When it comes to the tea itself, we don't make any compromises. The favorable climate and altitude in this Northeastern part of India is a suitable environment for black tea to grown. This ingredient also costs us millions of dollars to export out of India due to the ingredient being extremely rare and of high quality and because of the large commission that the locals get from us.

Purified Water from Reichenbach Falls in Meiringen, Switzerland

And of course, our most important ingredient comes from the Swiss alps all the way over to Meiringen. The water we use doesn't really differ that much from more practical sources of water, however, Le Gout de Leau is all about preserving "the essence of water". Though it is rarely used for some of our beverages, even just washing our fruits and other ingredients with it has become somewhat of a sacred ritual for us. It's all about the philosophy behind the brewing and preparation process which defines who we are and why are drinks are so successful.
